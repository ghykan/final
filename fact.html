Livestock-based food production is an important and pervasive way humans impact the environment. It causes about one-fifth of global greenhouse gas emissions, and is the key land user and source of water pollution by nutrient overabundance.

Beef production requires 28, 11, 5, and 6 times more land, irrigation water, GHG, and Nr, respectively, than the average of the other livestock categories. Cattle pass a lot of gas, and the methane from their flatulence and especially, their belches, is an expanding burden on the planet.

A cow does on overage release between 70 and 120 kg of Methane per year. The negative effect on the climate of Methane is 23 times higher than the effect of CO2.

Therefore the release of about 100 kg Methane per year for each cow is equivalent to about 2'300 kg CO2 per year. Livestock account for 14.5 percent of all.
